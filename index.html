Countdown to Impact: A Timeline

13.8 Billion years ago - The Big Bang

The Big Bang is a scientific theory about how the universe started, and then made the stars and galaxies we see today. The Big Bang is the name that scientists use for the most common theory of the universe, from the very early stages to the present day.

12.6 Billion years ago - Milky Way Forms

The Milky Way is our home galaxy. It contains around 400 billion stars, including our Sun. The Milky Way has a diameter of about 170,000 or 200,000 light years, and is a barred spiral galaxy.

6.5 Billion years ago - Milky Way starts spiraling

The magnetic field alignment with the star formation implies that the gravitational forces that created the galaxy's spiral shape is also compressing the magnetic field.

5.5 Billion years ago - Solar nebula starts collapsing

A shock wave from a nearby supernova explosion probably initiated the collapse of the solar nebula. The Sun formed in the center, and the planets formed in a thin disk orbiting around it. In a similar manner, moons formed orbiting the gas giant planets.

4.57 Billion years ago - The sun forms

The Sun formed about 4.6 billion years ago in a giant, spinning cloud of gas and dust called the solar nebula. As the nebula collapsed under its own gravity, it spun faster and flattened into a disk.

4.4 Billion years ago - Oceans form on earth

Water remained a gas until the Earth cooled below 100C . At this time, about 4.4 billion years ago, the water condensed into rain which filled the basins that we now know as our world ocean.

4.2 Billion years ago - Life emerges on earth

The earliest life forms we know of were microscopic organisms (microbes) that left signals of their presence in rocks about 4.2 billion years old. The signals consisted of a type of carbon molecule that is produced by living things.

2.1 Billion years ago - Earliest multicellular life develops

The earliest fossils of multicellular organisms include the contested Grypania spiralis and the fossils of the black shales formation in Gabon.

420 Million years ago - First air breathing animals develop

Pneumodesmus newmani is a species of myriapod that lived in the Paleozoic. It is one of the first myriapods, and among the oldest creatures to have lived on land.

300 Million years ago - Pangaea forms

About 320 million years ago, there was a major collision, geologically speaking, "when Gondwana, Laurussia, and intervening terranes collided to form the Pangea supercontinent".

231 Million years ago - First dinosaurs develop

Approximately 230 million years ago, during the Triassic Period, the dinosaurs appeared, evolved from the reptiles. Plateosaurus was one of the first large planteating dinosaurs, a relative of the much larger sauropods. It grew to about 9 meters in length.

65 Million ears ago - Asteroid Impact

According to abundant geological evidence, an asteroid roughly 10 km (6 miles) across hit Earth about 65 million years ago. This impact made a huge explosion and a crater about 180 km (roughly 110 miles) across. The immense impact and the resulting climatic changes ultimately resulted in the demise of the dinosaurs.
